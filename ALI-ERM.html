2. Understanding GenAI & Agentic AI for Risk Management

2.1 Generative AI (GenAI)

Large Language Models (LLMs) that understand context, generate human-quality text, and reason through complex problems:

  • Document Intelligence: Process contracts, policies, audit reports, and regulatory filings at scale
  • Report Generation: Auto-create board presentations, risk summaries, and compliance disclosures
  • Conversational Interface: Query risk data in natural language ("What are our top cyber risks?")
  • Scenario Narratives: Generate detailed risk scenarios for stress testing

2.2 Agentic AI

Autonomous AI agents that can plan, execute multi-step tasks, use tools, and take actions:

  • Continuous Monitoring: Agents scan news, social media, and market data 24/7 for emerging risks
  • Workflow Automation: Trigger actions automatically when risk thresholds are breached
  • Multi-Agent Collaboration: Specialized agents work together on complex risk analysis
  • Self-Improvement: Systems learn from outcomes and refine risk models over time

13. Governance & Risk Management for AI

13.1 AI Risk Framework

Deploying AI introduces its own risks that must be managed:

  • Model Risk: Validation, testing, performance monitoring, bias detection
  • Data Risk: Quality, privacy, security, lineage, regulatory compliance
  • Operational Risk: Availability, accuracy, failure modes, recovery
  • Ethical Risk: Fairness, transparency, accountability, human oversight

13.2 Responsible AI Principles

  1. Transparency: All AI decisions are explainable with clear reasoning
  2. Human Oversight: Critical decisions require human approval
  3. Auditability: Complete audit trail of all AI recommendations and actions
  4. Bias Mitigation: Regular testing for bias in AI outputs
  5. Privacy by Design: Data minimization, encryption, access controls
  6. Continuous Improvement: Feedback loops to improve AI accuracy over time

13.3 Governance Structure

  • AI Steering Committee: Executive oversight of AI initiatives (quarterly)
  • Model Risk Management: Validation and monitoring of all AI models
  • Data Governance: Policies for data quality, access, and privacy
  • Ethics Review: Assessment of AI applications for ethical concerns
